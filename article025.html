Protein Fudge Brownies

cal
9

carb

Christina Hallalulu
2015-02-27
Healthy brownies, an oxymoron? Not anymore! Made with peanut butter, protein powder, and chickpeas (say whaaat?!), this Lila, Chop! recipe might just be the most clandestinely healthy thing I've ever made. (Don't let the chickpeas dissuade you, you'd never know the difference.)

These taste exactly like normal brownies, I don't believe they're healthy at all, said the wonderful K, my recipe-taster and website creator.

While healthy brownies might seem an oxymoron, I can assure you these are just about the most clandestinely healthy thing I've ever made. The secret ingredient is beans (say whaaat?!) – chickpeas in fact, also known as garbanzo beans in the US. I know, I know, beans in brownies does sound a little funny. I thought so too when I first heard of it, and avoided all bean-containing recipes until I discovered this delicious-looking recipe for Melting Heart Protein Brownies. Gosh, am I glad I decided to give baking with beans a try! These turned out so fabulously moist and chewy from the chickpeas, fudgey from the peanut butter, and chocolate-rich from the cacao and protein powder. The best part was sticking little squares of sugar-free chocolate into each brownie when it was still hot from the oven, making the chocolate melt into the centre and giving it an fondant-like effect.

I would never have been able to tell the difference, said K after I revealed secret ingredient to him. In my chocolate-connoisseur's opinion, they were so delicious it was almost indecent. Yup, these healthy protein treats taste better than the real thing and might just be the best Lila, Chop! recipe I've ever made.

Servings: (Total Calories: .)
Steps
time instruction
3 min Blend all the ingredients together using a hand-held blender or in a high-speed mixer.
Pour batter into a brownie pan or baking dish lined with parchment paper.
18-20 min Bake at 175'C (350'F) or until tooth-pick comes out clean when you poke the the brownie.
Cut the brownie into 10 squares when it's still hot from the oven.
Stick 1 chocolate square in the middle of each brownie square, and watch the heat from the newly-baked brownies melt the chocolate.
25 min Happily devour with an optional scoop of low-fat vanilla ice-cream or enjoy with a cup of coffee

I heart Peanut butter (Peanut Butter & Co. product review)

There are so many different peanut butters available on the market these days, ranging from the original Skippy to organic peanuts-only spread that you grind yourself in Whole Foods, to to decadent protein- and vitamin-fortified peanut butters. The flavour combination seems limitless – gone are my childhood days when it was just the ol' PB&J combo on whole-wheat toast (minus the crusts, of course), that I would eat every day after coming home from school. I recently received free samples of three different flavours from Peanut Butter & Co. (thank you!) , and immediately fell in love with their White Chocolate Wonderful flavour – a creamy and delicious blend of peanuts and white chocolate.

Made with entirely natural ingredients and containing no cholesterol, trans fats, hydrogenated oils, or high-fructose corn syrup, these peanut butters are gluten-free, vegan, kosher, and truly delicious. Eat it on toast, crackers, together with fruit, or with a spoon straight out of the jar. The best part: it works fabulously in this Lila, Chop! protein fudge brownie recipe! With a whole range of flavours, I can't wait to try their cinnamon raisin swirl or dark chocolate dreams (can you tell I love chocolate?).

Pimp my Brownie Recipe Tip

Why not try pairing this Protein Fudge Brownie with a PB Fudge Protein Cookie? Because cookies + brownies = crownies, and no one ever said you can't have both (especially when they're both clean and healthy Lila, Chop! protein recipes).
